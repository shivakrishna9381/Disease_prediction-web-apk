1. Risk Factors for Diabetes:

  • Age: Individuals over 45 are at a higher risk, though diabetes can develop at any age.
  • Family History: A family history of diabetes (particularly Type 2) significantly increases the risk.
  • Obesity and Body Fat Distribution: Excess weight, especially around the abdomen, raises insulin resistance, increasing the likelihood of developing Type 2 diabetes.
  • Sedentary Lifestyle: Lack of physical activity can contribute to obesity and insulin resistance.
  • High Blood Pressure and Cholesterol Levels: Hypertension and abnormal cholesterol levels are often associated with insulin resistance and increase diabetes risk.
  • Gestational Diabetes: Women who have had gestational diabetes or delivered a baby weighing more than 9 pounds are at higher risk of developing Type 2 diabetes later in life.
  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS): PCOS is associated with insulin resistance, which can increase the risk of diabetes.

2. Lifestyle Recommendations:

  • Balanced Diet: Emphasize whole grains, fruits, vegetables, lean proteins, and healthy fats. Limit refined sugars and simple carbs that cause spikes in blood sugar.
  • Regular Physical Activity: Aim for 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise weekly, such as brisk walking or swimming, combined with muscle-strengthening activities to improve insulin sensitivity.
  • Weight Management: Losing even 5-10% of body weight if overweight can significantly reduce diabetes risk.
  • Stress Management: High stress can increase blood sugar levels. Mindfulness, relaxation techniques, and regular physical activity can support stress reduction.
  • Limit Alcohol and Avoid Smoking: Smoking and high alcohol intake can increase blood sugar levels and complicate diabetes management.
